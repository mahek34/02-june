1-List all 6 phases of SDLC in order with one line about each.
>Here are the 6 phases of SDLC in order:
1. Planning – The project goals, scope, timeline, and budget are defined.
2. Requirements Analysis – User needs and system requirements are collected and analyzed.
3. Design – The system architecture, database, and UI design are created.
4. Implementation (Development) – Developers write code to build the actual software.
5. Testing – The software is tested to find and fix bugs and ensure quality.
6. Maintenance – After deployment, updates, fixes, and improvements are made.

2-Write 3 differences between Agile and Waterfall models.
>Here are 3 differences between Agile and Waterfall models:
1. Process style – Agile is iterative and flexible, while Waterfall is linear and follows a step-by-step flow.
2. Changes – In Agile, requirements can change at any stage, but in Waterfall, changes are difficult once the process has started.
3. Delivery – Agile delivers the product in small parts (iterations), while Waterfall delivers the complete product at the end.

3-Name the 3 main Scrum roles and write one line about the responsibility of each.
>Here are the 3 main Scrum roles :
1. Product Owner – Responsible for defining the product vision and managing the product backlog.
2. Scrum Master – Ensures the Scrum process is followed properly and helps remove any blockers for the team.
3. Development Team – A group of professionals who design, build, test, and deliver the product increment.

4-What is a Sprint in Scrum? Mention its typical duration.
>A Sprint in Scrum is a fixed time period during which a team works to complete a specific set of tasks and deliver a usable product increment.
>Typical duration: Usually 1 to 4 weeks(most commonly 2 weeks).

5-What do these Jira board columns mean: To Do, In Progress, Done, Backlog?
>Here’s what Jira board columns mean :
1. Backlog – All tasks/user stories that are planned but not yet picked for work.
2. To Do – Tasks that are selected for the current sprint and ready to start.
3. In Progress – Tasks that are currently being worked on by the team.
4. Done – Tasks that are completed, tested, and finished.
